Indian food is known for its rich spices, bold flavors, and colorful dishes. From creamy curries to crispy street snacks, every region offers something unique and deeply rooted in tradition.

About These Dishes

Biryani

Fragrant rice layered with spiced meat or vegetables, slow-cooked to absorb rich flavors. Known for its aroma and saffron color.

Butter Chicken

Creamy tomato-based curry with tender chicken pieces. Mild, rich, and slightly sweet, popular worldwide.

Garlic Naan

Soft flatbread baked in a tandoor, topped with garlic and butter. Perfect for scooping up curries.

Tandoori Chicken

Chicken marinated in yogurt and spices, roasted in a tandoor. Smoky, juicy, and brightly colored.

Dal Makhani

Slow-cooked black lentils with butter and cream. Rich, comforting, and full of flavor.

Palak Paneer

Spinach curry with soft paneer cubes. Mild, creamy, and nutritious, often eaten with naan or rice.

Pakora

Crispy vegetable fritters coated in spiced chickpea flour. Popular street snack, especially during rain.

Pani Puri

Hollow crispy shells filled with spiced water, potatoes, and chickpeas. Tangy, spicy, and refreshing.

Chicken Tikka Masala

Grilled chicken pieces in spiced tomato and cream sauce. Bold, rich, and slightly smoky.

Gulab Jamun

Deep-fried milk dough balls soaked in rose-flavored sugar syrup. Sweet and melt-in-your-mouth dessert.
